The Wheel

It does not ask. It does not explain. It turns.

“

No king commands it. No priest owns it. No scholar understands it. Yet when the Wheel turns, even empires listen.

― High Scribe Maelor Venn
What Is the Wheel of Origin?

The Wheel of Origin is the oldest and most disputed mystery in the known world. To some, it is a divine instrument. To others, it is an ancient machine, a cosmic law, a prison of fate, or a weapon left behind by powers no longer remembered.

Most people never see the Wheel awaken. They know it only through stories, temple sermons, tavern songs, forbidden books, and the names of champions carved into old stone.

But when the Wheel turns, it does not merely predict fate. It reveals it.

During a Grand Ordeal, the Wheel calls champions from across the world. It reveals their race, origin, profession, weapon, armor, companion, power, enchantment, blessing, curse, and mastery — all recorded by the Scribes of Origin as official Champion Records. No one knows why the Wheel chooses one soul and ignores another.

The Crownspire Manifestation

In Crownspire, capital of Elarion, the Wheel appears through an ancient structure known as the Ring of Origin. It stands at the heart of the Grand Ordeal arena, surrounded by stone channels, bronze spokes, black glass, and runes older than the city itself.

The Ring is not believed to be the Wheel in its entirety. Most scholars agree it is only a manifestation — a place where the unseen force can touch the physical world.

When dormant, the Ring is silent. When awakened, its symbols burn with gold, violet, white, and shadowed light. The air bends. Names appear in languages no living scribe remembers.

The Champion Revealing

Every spin is a record. Every record becomes fate.

I
The Call
A potential champion is marked by dreams, omens, symbols, whispers, wounds, coins, contracts, visions, or sudden changes in power. Some feel drawn toward Crownspire. Others are found by scouts, priests, guilds, or enemies.
II
The Arrival
Champions arrive at the host city by road, ship, escort, chains, magic, or accident. Some come willingly. Some are forced. Some simply appear where no door opened.
III
The Turning
Before the Scribes of Origin, the Ring of Origin awakens. Its symbols turn, fracture, align, or burn. The champion's fate is revealed piece by piece.
IV
The Record
Race, subrace, profession, weapon, armor, companion, power, enchantment, blessing or curse, and mastery are recorded into the Atlas. If something cannot be read, the Scribes mark it as Unknown, Unrecorded, Forbidden, or ???
V
The Oath of Entry
Once recorded, the champion becomes part of the Grand Ordeal. Their name enters the bracket. From that moment on, they are no longer only a person. They are a piece of history in motion.

What the World Believes

The Wheel has many witnesses, but no single truth.

☣
The Church of the First Spin
The Church teaches that the Wheel is sacred will made visible. To them, every champion is chosen for a reason — even if that reason is painful, monstrous, or impossible to understand.
📚
The Scribes of Origin
The Scribes do not worship the Wheel. They record it. Their duty is to observe, classify, preserve, and question. Some Scribes secretly believe the Wheel is becoming unstable.
⚙️
The Iron Covenant
Artificers, Mechanoids, and engineers argue that the Wheel may be a machine — impossibly ancient, but still a system. And every system can be studied, repaired, or broken.
☠
The Broken Spoke
Rebels, outcasts, and cursed wanderers believe the Wheel is not salvation, but control. To them, being chosen is not an honor. It is a sentence.
Fate-Marks

Those touched by the Wheel often bear signs called Fate-Marks. These can appear as glowing scars, shifting symbols, strange tattoos, changes in eye color, unnatural dreams, or invisible wounds only certain magic can detect.

A Fate-Mark does not always mean someone will become a champion. Sometimes it means they were considered. Sometimes it means they were rejected. Sometimes it means the Wheel tried to choose them — and failed.

Known Fate-Mark Signs
◆ Burning wheel-shaped scars
◆ Dreams of a golden ring
◆ Hearing one's name in an empty room
◆ Weapons appearing in reflections
◆ Unreadable symbols on the skin
◆ Animals refusing to approach
◆ Sudden changes in magical power
◆ Repeating the same day, phrase, or memory
◆ A shadow that moves half a second too late
Unknown Readings

Most Revealings are clear enough to be recorded. But sometimes the Wheel does not provide answers. When the Scribes cannot identify a champion's origin, homeland, bloodline, power source, or classification, they use restricted markings:

Unknown
The information could not be read.
Unrecorded
The information exists, but no known archive matches it.
Forbidden
The information was sealed by ancient law, priestly order, or Scribe authority.
???
The Wheel produced symbols, sounds, or distortions that cannot be translated.

Glitch Entities, certain Voidborn, ancient Titans, and corrupted fate-lines are often connected to such readings.

Signs the Wheel May Be Damaged

In older chronicles, the Wheel is described as terrible but precise. In the current age, that certainty has begun to weaken.

During the preparations for the Grand Ordeal of Crownspire, Scribes have reported errors that should not be possible:

Names appearing twice
Origins marked as ???
Race symbols flickering between classifications
Fate-Marks forming on people never chosen
Dead champions appearing in minor records
Spokes of the Ring moving while dormant
Shadows inside the Ring turning against the light
Unwritten pages filling with warnings

No public announcement has been made.  ·  The city continues to celebrate.  ·  The Wheel continues to turn.

⚠ The Scribe's Warning

A private note found in the sealed margins of the Crownspire Ledger reads:

“If the Wheel is choosing wrongly, then the Ordeal will not crown a champion.
It will open a wound.”
